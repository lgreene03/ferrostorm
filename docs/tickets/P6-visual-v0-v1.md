## Plan comment, written before starting

**Approach.** Build the harness first and change nothing until it works, because
doc 25's whole argument is that four visual waves shipped without anyone looking
at a frame. Then take the two one-line day-one experiments and keep the images.
Then land V1-01 through V1-06 as a single change, because several of them look
wrong alone, judge that on a contact sheet, and only then make V1-07's single
retune with the capture in front of me.

**Assumptions.** That doc 22 remains ratified and C-08's values stand as
written. That the reference save may be generated by driving both sides with
the Standard AI rather than by playing, since a played save leaves one base a
stub. That hiding the HUD during capture is correct, because every measurement
in LOOK-02 is about the battlefield and a sidebar occupying a fifth of the frame
would dominate all of them.

**Interfaces touched.** `SkirmishLive.AutoStep` and `SkirmishLive.StepTicks`,
both of which already exist as the offscreen-verification surface, read only.
`BattlefieldView.BuildEnvironment`. `FogOfWar.UpdateFrom`. The ground shader's
sampler declarations. project.godot's `[rendering]` section. Nothing under
/sim, and nothing that reads or writes simulation state.

## What shipped

### Wave V0, the look-development loop

**LOOK-01, the capture harness.** `game/scripts/LookDev.cs` plus
`game/scenes/LookDev.tscn`, driven by `tools/lookdev/capture.sh`. Committed, not
a throwaway autoload, because the point is that the same three frames can be
taken again in six months and still be comparable.

- One fixed reference scene: `tools/lookdev/reference-state.fsav`, committed, a
  skirmish-03 state at tick 3600 from seed 2026 with 12000 starting credits and
  both sides driven by the Standard AI. Both bases are built out, eight
  Directorate and eleven Sodality units are alive, a vehicle from each side sits
  on the western bridge, and player 0's visibility divides the map into 599
  visible cells, 1652 explored-but-unseen and 3893 unexplored, so all three
  shroud states are in shot. Regenerate with
  `tools/lookdev/make-reference-save.sh`, which should almost never be needed.
- Three frozen cameras at the shipped -50 pitch, constants recorded in the
  source with an instruction never to move them. CAM-A ground (40, 30) at height
  22. CAM-B ground (48, 30) at height 42, framed so both bases are in one shot.
  CAM-C ground (24, 31) at height 9, on the bridge where a Directorate and a
  Sodality vehicle sit a few metres apart over water.
- It captures through the SKIRMISH path. It instantiates `res://scenes/Skirmish.tscn`,
  the scene the menu launches, and never touches Battle3D.tscn or ReplayTheater,
  which between them light a replay with five directionals totalling 4.55
  energy.
- Three locks against running in a shipped session: it is not an autoload, it is
  in no shipped scene tree, and the script quits with an error unless
  `--lookdev` is on the command line.
- `--lookdev-off=ssao,ssil,ssr,glow,fog,dof,particles` A/Bs any single pass. This
  was added to chase a determinism bug and kept because it is the tool that
  turns "I think it is the fog" into an image.

**A correction to the invocation the roadmap specifies.** LOOK-01 clause 4 says
headless. Godot's `--headless` selects the dummy rasterizer and renders nothing,
so a headless capture is a blank PNG. The harness runs windowed with
`--audio-driver Dummy`, which is the flag that genuinely matters offscreen, plus
`--fixed-fps 60` so the delta handed to tweens and particles is the same on any
machine.

**LOOK-02, the contact sheet and the table.** `tools/lookdev/contact.py`,
standard library plus Pillow. Three rows, before left and after right, plus a
printed table of mean luminance, mean HSV saturation, the 5th and 95th luminance
percentiles and the hue span, under four masks. **Every table prints its mask
and the exact rectangles that mask is made of**, because doc 25 records two
audits reaching opposite conclusions about the same asset by silently disagreeing
about which pixels counted. Acceptance met: run against two identical
directories, every delta prints as exactly 0.000.

The four masks are `frame` (every pixel), `scene` (every pixel that is not pure
black), `ground` (hand-placed open-ground rectangles per camera) and `unit`
(hand-placed hardware rectangles per camera). Two notes that came out of using
it:

- At baseline `scene` and `frame` select the same pixels, because nothing in the
  frame is pure black: the off-map void still shows the procedural sky. They
  diverge the moment the fog is cut, which is itself a finding.
- The hue-span metric needs a chroma floor AND a value floor. With a chroma
  floor alone the CAM-A hardware mask reported a 314-degree hue span, which was
  near-black pixels whose hue is quantisation noise.

### The determinism story, because it cost the most

Acceptance asks for two consecutive runs producing byte-identical PNGs. What was
found:

1. The sim must not run off the frame clock. `SkirmishLive.AutoStep` is set false
   and the world is stepped exactly twice, explicitly. Two rather than zero
   because `FogOfWar` only rebuilds its texture inside `RunOneTick`, so a freshly
   loaded save renders a fully unexplored shroud until the sim steps.
2. The warm-up must be counted in PROCESS frames, not rendered frames. Turret
   sway, track scroll, wheel spin, the infantry stride and the water UV scroll
   all accumulate the delta `_Process` is handed. Counting rendered frames put
   the pause one process frame either side of where it meant to be and moved a
   tank's tracks by a fraction of a texel.
3. Particle systems take a random seed at construction. They are seeded and
   restarted AFTER thirty frames, not before, because SkirmishLive builds a
   damage-smoke emitter per wounded structure inside `SyncActors` and a seeding
   pass done earlier misses every one of them. Before this, runs differed by
   about a thousand pixels around the burning buildings and nowhere else.
4. Thirty settle frames is not enough. The roadmap asks for thirty; the fog's
   temporal reprojection has not converged at thirty and the capture came back
   byte-different about half the time. 240 frames converges past the point where
   it changes a byte.
5. **One process per camera.** The fog's temporal history is a property of every
   frame the process has drawn, so capturing three cameras in one process gives
   the second a history containing the first. Returning to CAM-A at the end of a
   three-camera run and shooting it again produced 16,592 differing pixels at up
   to 64/255 with the scene frozen. That measurement is what identified the
   cause.

**What is left, stated honestly.** With SSAO, SSIL, SSR, the glow pass, the
volumetric fog, the depth of field and every particle system disabled, six
consecutive runs are byte-identical. That is the proof that everything the
harness owns is frozen. Switch any ONE of those passes back on and a handful of
pixels take one of two values, always in the same tight cluster on one
high-contrast vehicle edge, and which pass is on changes nothing about which
pixels move. It is a floating-point ordering difference in the driver's compute
passes landing on a pixel that already sat on a rounding boundary. Runs are
frequently byte-identical on all three cameras and occasionally differ on up to
19 pixels of 1,440,000.

`tools/lookdev/verify-determinism.sh` gates on a count and a whole-frame mean
rather than a per-pixel ceiling, and the reason is that a per-pixel ceiling is
not stable under grading: the same pixels differed by at most 4/255 before Wave
V1 and by up to 35/255 after V1 raised the exposure, with nothing about the
capture changed. The gate is 200 pixels and a whole-frame mean absolute
difference below 0.01 of one level. The smallest ticket in this wave moved
385,000 pixels, so the floor cannot hide a real change.

### The day-one experiments

Both were captured before any change landed and both were reverted. Numbers at
CAM-A, whole frame, against the baseline.

| | mean luminance | mean HSV sat | hue span | ground mean |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| baseline | 55.0 | 0.488 | 18.5 deg | 78.0 |
| fog density 0 | 17.5 (-37.5) | 0.064 (-0.424) | 222.7 deg (+204) | 40.8 (-37.2) |
| shroud hidden | 62.5 (+7.5) | 0.424 (-0.064) | 18.5 deg (0.0) | 85.1 (+7.1) |

**The fog is the dominant veil and it is not close.** It was ADDING 37.5 levels
of uniform luminance to every pixel at CAM-A and holding the entire battlefield
inside an 18-degree hue span; without it the frame spans 223 degrees. At CAM-B,
where the minimum camera distance is 54.8 m, it was adding 40.4 levels and the
open-ground mean fell from 69.2 to 4.3 without it.

**It also settles a metric.** The frame's apparent saturation was the fog's blue
cast, not the art's chroma: cutting the fog COLLAPSES mean HSV saturation from
0.488 to 0.064 while transforming the image into something with real colour in
it. V1-01's stated acceptance criterion, "mean HSV saturation at CAM-A rises by
at least 0.03 absolute", is therefore wrong-signed. Hue span is the metric that
tracks what a viewer sees. This is recorded rather than quietly worked around.

**The shroud is real but second order**, worth about 7.5 levels at CAM-A with no
effect on hue. Its visible sin is the large soft dark smears across the frame,
not a colour cast, and those smears are invisible in the baseline only because
the fog was lifting them.

## What each change did to the frame, having looked at it

**The fog cut is the whole wave.** At the baseline every one of the three
captures is a flat blue-grey wash in which the ground has no material, the hulls
are chalky plastic and the only things with any identity are the ferrite glow
and the team marks. After the cut the ground reads as warm dusty terrain with
visible grain, rocks, splat boundaries, cliff strata and scatter; the ruins,
the fence lines and the hills all resolve as separate things; the river reads as
water rather than as a slightly different grey. At CAM-B, where the baseline is
close to a single flat colour across the whole map, the entire battlefield now
has readable terrain.

**The shroud change is honest but it exposed something.** With the fog no longer
lifting it, the explored-but-unseen region reads much darker relative to the
visible ground than it did, and the boundary between the two is now visibly
BLOCKY: the shroud texture is one texel per map cell, so at CAM-A each cell is
about thirty pixels and the linear filter cannot hide the staircase. This is a
new visible defect that V1-01 uncovered rather than caused, and it is not in
scope here. It is the first thing I would fix next.

**Sky-sourced ambient did what it was supposed to on structures and did not
prove itself on the criterion as written.** Panel tops, deck faces and the tops
of the ruin blocks separate from their sides in a way they did not before, and
the hulls have a top-lit read instead of a uniform one. But V1-03's stated
measurement, "sample an upward-facing and a downward-facing hull face on the
same tank in shadow", is not measurable at this camera: at a fixed -50 pitch no
downward-facing face is visible at all, and there is no tank in shadow in the
reference state. Substituting the top deck against the near-vertical skirt on
the Directorate hull gives a separation of 10.3/255 before and 9.7/255 after,
which does not demonstrate the ticket either way. Recorded as unmet-because-
unmeasurable rather than dressed up.

**Anisotropic filtering is real and smaller than advertised.** Isolated by
reverting only the three sampler hints and re-capturing an otherwise identical
tree, Laplacian variance over the middle third rises 12.8 per cent at CAM-A and
1.9 per cent at CAM-B, against a criterion of 15 per cent. The splat boundaries
and cliff strata do resolve slightly further out. It stays, because it is free
and because it is the fix that actually reaches the ground, but the criterion is
not met.

**The project rendering keys are visibly doing something**, which is worth
stating because doc 25 section 6 records a version of this ticket that would
have been a verified no-op. Isolated the same way, they change 415,739 pixels of
1,440,000 at CAM-A with a maximum delta of 160/255. The specific banding
criterion, "count distinct luminance steps across a 200-pixel sky run at CAM-B
and at least double it", goes from 3 to 4: the run it names spans only three
levels in the first place, so the metric has no resolution to show the change.

**SSR removal costs nothing visible.** No regression at any of the three
cameras, and the water keeps its sky reflection.

**V1-07 was a lift, not a cut, and this is the part to read carefully.** The
roadmap is emphatic that the frame is already too dark and that ambient must not
be dropped. After V1-01 through V1-06 the mean open-ground luminance at CAM-A
had fallen from 78 to 55 out of 255, because the fog had been ADDING uniform
brightness and cutting it takes that away. Two things then came out of trying to
lift it:

- **`AmbientLightEnergy` is dead under the configuration V1-03 mandates.**
  Raising it from 0.4 to 1.0 moved mean open-ground luminance by 0.03 out of
  255. Under `AmbientSource.Sky` with `AmbientLightSkyContribution` at 1.0, the
  ambient level is set entirely by the sky colours and `SkyEnergyMultiplier`.
  V1-07's own candidate knob therefore does not exist. The value is left at 0.4
  per V1-03 clause 4 rather than deleted, with a comment saying it is inert.
- The single adjustment made instead is `TonemapExposure`, which doc 22's C-10
  clause 6 already names as the one permitted compensating knob and requires be
  recorded. It multiplies where the fog added, so it restores the level without
  re-flattening the contrast the fog cut just bought, and it leaves the key,
  fill, rim and ambient balance untouched for C-10 to retune. Measured: 1.9 gave
  71, 2.4 gave 83, 2.9 gives 93.8, which is inside doc 22's ratified 90 to 135
  band. **The baseline was 78 and never met that band either.**

## Frame time

Measured at CAM-B with both bases in view, 1600x900, V-Sync forced off, as the
mean wall-clock period over 240 rendered frames.

| | frame time | draw calls |
|---|---|---|
| baseline | 10.27 ms (10.19 to 10.47 over five runs) | 117 |
| after V1 | 9.86 ms (9.83 to 9.86) | 117 |

About 0.41 ms faster, roughly four per cent, which is the direction doc 25
section 5 predicts: the fog density cut and the removal of 56-step SSR more than
pay for FXAA, debanding and 16x anisotropic filtering. This is the project's
first GPU-side number of any kind.
