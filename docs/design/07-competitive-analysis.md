# 07 - Competitive Analysis and Market Positioning

Version 0.1, researched July 2026. Purpose: validate risk R6 (market viability) before major spend, and define the gap this game fills. Sources: Steam data, press coverage, revenue estimators - figures are third-party estimates, not audited numbers.

---

## 1. The headline finding

The direct comparable already exists and it succeeded. **Tempest Rising** (Slipgate Ironworks, April 2025, $39.99) is an explicitly C&C-style RTS: MCV base-building, power management, two asymmetric factions (third added later), twin campaigns, Glicko-2 ranked ladder. Reception: ~88% positive across ~9,500+ Steam reviews, Metascore 80, a Game Awards strategy nomination, and third-party gross revenue estimates around $10-11M (developer net ~$3M est.). It charted among Steam's top sellers in its launch month against heavyweight releases.

**Implications for us:**
1. **The market is proven.** "Classic C&C-style with modern production" is a validated commercial category, not a nostalgia fantasy. R6 downgrades from "is there a market?" to "is there room for a second entrant at our scale?"
2. **The bar is set.** Tempest Rising launched feature-complete with voiced campaigns and high production polish, backed by publishers. We cannot out-produce it; we must out-position it.
3. **Its gaps are our openings** (see §3).

## 3. Where the gaps are (differentiation thesis)

Tempest Rising's known soft spots and structural choices leave openings:

1. **Modding and community content.** TR launched without deep mod tooling or Workshop-first design. Our data-driven files, shipped map editor, and format-stability promise target the P4 persona and the longevity OpenRA proves is possible. **This is the primary differentiator.**
2. **Determinism-first spectator/replay ecosystem.** Full replays, observer tooling, and casting features as launch pillars, not patches - courting the P6 creator persona early is cheap marketing.
3. **Preservation promise.** "Playable forever, LAN and direct-connect, no dead-server orphaning" is a loud, credible pledge an indie can make and publishers rarely do. It resonates precisely with the C&C generation, who watched their games' servers die.
4. **Faster, meaner games.** TR skews toward longer, macro-heavy matches; we deliberately tune for the 15-30 minute lethal classic pace (GDD pillar 2). A sharper feel, not a broader one.
5. **Price tier.** Launch at £19.99-24.99 / $24.99-29.99 - under TR, over shovelware - positioned as the tight, moddable enthusiast entry rather than the production-value flagship.

What we do NOT try to win on: campaign production value, art spectacle, faction count, marketing spend.

## 4. Threats specific to us

- **OpenRA is free and beloved.** Any P1 veteran can get the classic feel for £0. Our answer must be "modern QoL + new content + active balance + it's *ours*", and the vertical slice must prove that answer.
- **Launch-window crowding.** Dawn of War IV (Sep 2026) and the indie revival wave will ration press and streamer attention through 2026-27. Our realistic launch is 2027+; monitor the field again at Alpha gate.
- **Fractured Alliance overlap.** Track it. If it ships first and well, our positioning may need to sharpen further toward modding/preservation; if it stumbles, its wishlist audience is reachable.
