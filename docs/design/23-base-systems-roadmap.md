# 23. Base Systems Roadmap: power, production buildings, unit placement, repair

Status: PROPOSED PLAN, implementation contract pending sign-off
Date: 2026-07-16
Owner: design-review, for execution by sim-engineer + client-engineer + ai-engineer + game-designer agents
Inputs: four parallel lens audits (power; production buildings and the tech tree; unit placement and spawning; repair), each then attacked by an independent sceptic who re-ran the evidence rather than re-reading the prose. Judged against docs/design/02-game-design-document.md, docs/design/03-technical-design-document.md, docs/adr/ADR-005-footprints-and-barriers.md, docs/design/21-ue5-portability-audit.md and docs/design/22-scale-and-colour-roadmap.md, at HEAD.

This document answers four questions Luke asked after a play-test: what about power usage, what about infantry barracks and vehicle factories or airfields, the placement of units does not work well, and what about repairing vehicles or units or buildings. Like doc 22 it is written to be executed by a cheaper model, so every ticket carries its full mechanical specification. Unlike doc 22 it leads with what already works, because three of the four areas are substantially built and the most expensive mistake available here is paying to rebuild them.

One editorial note, the same one doc 22 carries. GDD lines 45 and 83 name a specific 90s franchise as shorthand. CLAUDE.md's Legal rule forbids exactly those terms in this repository with no exemption for quotation, so where this document quotes those lines the trademark is elided and the elision is marked in place. The reasoning is preserved in full; the trademarks are not. The approved formulation is "inspired by the classic RTS games of the 90s".

A second note on method, which matters for how much of this to trust. Every auditor claim below survived an adversarial pass. Where a sceptic disproved an auditor, the sceptic won, and the corrected claim is what appears here. Section 7 is the ledger of what was dropped and why, and it is not decoration: several confidently written proposals from the audits would have shipped real damage, and one of them would have drained the treasury at three thousand credits a second. Anyone tempted to reach past this document into the raw audits should read section 7 first.

## 1. The straight answers

### What about power usage?

The power substrate is real, correct, per player, hashed and already fed from /data. Do not rebuild it. Entity carries PowerSupply and PowerDraw as plain integers (World.cs:81-82), both are hashed (World.cs:1811) and both survive save and load (World.Serialization.cs:144 and :165). Four couplings are live and three of them were proven by execution rather than by reading. Production speed scales exactly as GDD s5 asks: `int rate = draw[p] <= 0 || supply[p] >= draw[p] ? 100 : 50 + 50 * supply[p] / draw[p];` at World.cs:1646-1648, which the shipped gate independently pins at 75 ticks powered and 150 ticks unpowered for a rifle squad. The superweapon charge pauses in a brown-out (World.cs:1622), the veil projector collapses in one (World.cs:1034), and the service depot stops mending in one (World.cs:1604). The client already draws the GDD's bar honestly, with the 75 per cent threshold in integer maths (Sidebar.cs:307), and the skirmish AI keeps its own tally and builds plants with a deliberate 40-point margin (SkirmishAI.cs:94-97).

What is wrong is not the plumbing, it is that power costs nothing and buys nothing. The Construction Yard draws 0 (com_construction_yard.yaml:8) and the refinery draws 0 (com_refinery.yaml:8), so the opening base a player actually builds draws 40 in total against a single 300-credit plant that supplies 100. One of every building in the whole nine-building catalogue totals 250, which is three plants and 900 credits, against a Construction Yard that alone costs 3000. Power is a rounding error on the build order. And it has no teeth at the other end either: CombatSystem spans World.cs:1129 to :1318 and contains not one reference to power in any form, so a turret at zero supply and a turret at five hundred per cent supply are bit-identical in behaviour. There is no radar anywhere in the project to go dark, either: `grep -rn -i radar sim/ game/scripts/ data/` returns nothing at all. So the power model grew where it was easy, in three systems the GDD never asked for, and stalled in the two it did. That is the cleanest one-line summary of the area.

### What about infantry barracks and vehicle factories or airfields?

You are right on every count, and this is the one area where the honest answer is that almost nothing exists. `grep -rni "barracks\|airfield\|aircraft" sim game data` returns nothing at all. `EntityKind` (World.cs:42) is the complete list of what the sim can be and it ends at `ServiceDepot = 10, Wall = 11`; there is no producer other than the Factory and the Construction Yard. The entire `Produce` handler is World.cs:843-853 and it checks exactly three things: that the producer is a Factory, that the unit costs more than nothing, and that it belongs to your faction. So one factory builds a rifle squad, an engineer, three tanks, a howitzer, a harvester and a 3000-credit MCV, at tick one, with nothing else standing.

The production loop underneath is genuinely good and should not be rebuilt. The sim already supports N independent queues keyed per producer id (`_queues`, World.cs:317), which is exactly the machinery a barracks needs; pay-as-you-build drains credits proportionally to progress and halts cleanly while broke, which makes the pro-rata refund exact for free; and placement is complete, so adding a producer costs one arm in the PlaceStructure switch (World.cs:785-795) rather than new machinery. The tech tree is likewise half-built rather than absent: every unit YAML already declares a `prerequisites` list, and DataLoader parses it at DataLoader.cs:129 and :170 and then drops it on the floor, because neither type definition has a field to hold it (UnitCatalogue.ToTypeDef at DataLoader.cs:194, StructureCatalogue.ToTypeDef at DataLoader.cs:251). That drop is documented in writing at DataLoader.cs:244-251 rather than hidden, and the reason given is right: authoring a prereq table there would be inventing a Game Designer sign-off that nobody has given.

There is one more thing in this area that nobody asked about and that you should know. The Veil Projector, the Sodality's signature building, cannot be built by any human player. The sidebar's `Structures` array (Sidebar.cs:18-31) has seven entries and it is not among them; `grep -rni veil game/scripts/` returns exactly one hit and it is a render-only mesh mapping at ModelLibrary.cs:23. Meanwhile the sim gates struct type 7 to the Sodality at World.cs:804, sod_veil_projector.yaml exists with a cost of 1500 and a draw of 60, the selftest proves its numbers, campaign.txt advertises "7 veil" as a grantable id, and the gate battery exercises it. The only way a veil projector reaches a match is a direct spawn from the scenario harness. It is a fully implemented building with no button.

### The placement of units does not work well.

This one has a specific, measured cause, and it is almost certainly what you saw. Every unit a factory ever builds spawns on the same cell, and they never move apart. The spawn loop at World.cs:1682-1694 tests only `!Map.InBounds(nx, ny) || Map.IsBlocked(nx, ny)`, and units never write the passability grid: `Map.SetBlocked` is called only from BlockFootprint and UnblockFootprint at World.cs:433 and :441, for structures. So the first offset in the ring, `(0, 2)`, is always free and always wins. Ten rifle squads produced back to back all sat at exactly (21.500, 23.500): one distinct position, minimum pairwise distance 0.0000, all forty-five pairs inside the 0.6-cell separation minimum. They stay there because SeparationSystem is gated on `!e.Moving` at World.cs:1433 and a freshly spawned unit is not moving. Ten tanks render as one tank.

The separation and crowd-arrival systems themselves are good and were debugged through three named failure modes; they are not the problem and should not be touched beyond two small corrections. What compounds the stacking is that nothing gives a produced unit a departure order, because there is no rally point in the sim at all. `grep -rni rally sim/` returns one hit and it is a comment. Rally lives entirely in a client dictionary (SkirmishLive.cs:147), which is why Q004 exists and is still undecided. And there is a second, nastier defect in the same twelve lines: `q.RemoveAt(0)` at World.cs:1680 fires before the spawn loop, and there is no else branch after it, so if every one of the eleven ring offsets is blocked the unit is deleted, the queue advances, the credits are gone, and no event fires. Wall yourself in tightly and your production vanishes silently.

Building placement, for the record, is fine. The ghost calls the sim's own `ValidPlacement` for its tint and the commit path calls the identical function, the anchor arithmetic round-trips exactly, and an audit that went looking for an off-by-one did not find one. The one lie is that the ghost tints green when you are broke or at the 80-wall cap, because those two tests live in the command handler at World.cs:754-755 and not in `ValidPlacement`.

### What about repairing vehicles or units or buildings?

Repair is not missing. It is one of the more complete systems in the sim, and it is completely invisible. This is the single most important paragraph in this document, because it is the place you are most likely to pay to rebuild something you already own.

Three repair paths already work and all three were proven by execution, twice, by two agents who did not share a harness. Structure repair works: select any building, press R, and it mends at 2 hp per tick for exactly 1 credit per tick, halting while you are broke and switching itself off when the building is whole (the command handler at World.cs:819-824, the loop at World.cs:1584-1594). It is a latching toggle, not a hold, and `Repairing` is hashed and survives save and load. The Service Depot works and is more forgiving than the classic idiom: it is an aura, not a drive-in pad. Any of your own damaged units or harvesters within four cells of a depot mend at the same 2 hp per tick for 1 credit each per tick, gated on your base being at or above full power (World.cs:1599-1618). It heals infantry exactly as readily as tanks, so there is no field hospital to build and the GDD never asks for one, and it heals units that are driving straight through without ever being told to park: a tank ordered through the aura healed on thirty-two consecutive ticks, every one of them a tick on which it was moving. And elite units self-repair for free, one hit point every fifteen ticks, forever, with no depot and no power (World.cs:1024), which is GDD line 54 implemented correctly.

So what is actually wrong is legibility, and one lie. Press R with tanks selected and the client filters them out at SkirmishLive.cs:1888 with `!Mobile(rv.Kind)`, so no command is sent, but `_audio.Play("ui_confirm", -8)` at SkirmishLive.cs:1890 sits outside that filter and fires anyway. The game plays the confirmation chime and does nothing. Meanwhile `grep -rn "Repairing" game/` returns zero hits: the client never reads the flag, so there is no wrench, no ring, no icon, no cost readout and no toast. Structure health bars are gated on `Mobile(hv.Kind)` at SkirmishLive.cs:1708, so the one class of thing you can repair is the one class with no health bar. Repair latches while you are broke and never clears, because World.cs:1587 skips the heal but leaves the flag set, so the drain starts later with no indicator. And at the real 80-wall cap a wall run costs 1200 credits per second to repair, next to a code comment at SkirmishLive.cs:1895-1896 reasoning that repair needs no confirmation guard because it is "reversible and cheap". It is reversible.

To use repair today, while Wave 1 is unwritten: select a building and press R, watch the credits fall, press R again to stop. For vehicles, build a Service Depot from the sidebar for 1200 credits and drive the damaged units within four cells of it. Wave 1 makes all of that legible without touching the simulation at all.

### If you fund one thing

Fund Wave 1. Fifteen tickets, entirely client-side, no golden hash moves, no ADR, no signature needed from anyone. It closes most of what you actually felt.

## 2. The finding nobody asked for: the shipped client never loads /data

This is the most consequential thing the whole exercise turned up, it is not in scope for any of the four questions, and it changes how several tickets below must be written. It was verified against the source before being written down, and it is worse than the audit that found it claimed.

`grep -rn "DataLoader\|RegisterUnitType\|RegisterStructureType" game/scripts/` returns nothing at all. Widened to the whole client tree and to the catalogue bridges, `grep -rn "DataLoader\.\|RegisterUnitType\|RegisterStructureType\|StructureCatalogue\.\|UnitCatalogue\." game/` still returns nothing. Every real caller of the /data path in this repository lives in sim/Ferrostorm.Sim.Runner/Program.cs, between lines 1582 and 1662, and that file is the gate harness. The client builds its world at SkirmishLive.cs:250 through `BuildStartingWorld` (SkirmishLive.cs:325-362), which grants credits, spawns two Construction Yards and a mirrored starting hand, and registers nothing at all.

The consequence is exact. A live match, and every LAN match, runs off the compiled catalogue: the `_unitTypes` dictionary at World.cs:289-308 and `SeedStructureTypes()` at World.cs:385-390. Editing the YAML does not change the game anyone plays. /data is currently a round-trip reference test, not the game's data source, and CLAUDE.md's data convention ("All gameplay numbers live in /data as YAML ... Hand-editing stats in code is forbidden") is in force in the gate and not in force in the shipped product.

Three things follow, and each one is load-bearing for a ticket below.

First, this is why the BD-06 "structure catalogue into /data" work was hash-neutral, and the hash-neutrality was not the achievement it looked like. Nothing that runs a match reads the files. The selftest proves the two agree; nothing makes them agree.

Second, any ticket that says "add a YAML file" is incomplete as written. The repair-vehicle proposal from the repair audit is the clearest case: author data/units/com_repair_vehicle.yaml, add a sim branch, and the vehicle does not exist in the game, because `GetUnitType(13)` returns `default` with Cost 0, Hp 0 and Speed 0 (World.cs:310 returns a defaulted struct rather than throwing). It must ALSO be added to the compiled `_unitTypes` at World.cs:289, which is literally the hand-editing of stats in code that CLAUDE.md forbids. That is not the implementer's contradiction to resolve. It is an ADR.

Third, the round-trip that is supposed to catch drift does not cover everything. The structure side walks the directory and checks all nine files with an explicit completeness loop, which is good. The unit side is hand-enumerated across Program.cs:1600-1621 and it loads eleven of the twelve unit files: `grep -rn "vanguard" sim/Ferrostorm.Sim.Runner/Program.cs` returns nothing, so unit type 12, the Vanguard Car, is the one catalogue entry in the game with no proof that its YAML matches its compiled def. Checked by hand, today they do agree. Nothing enforces that they continue to.

The recommendation is ticket P5-DATA-01 and it is an ADR, not a patch. The short version: make the client load /data before tick 0 exactly as the runner does, using the `GameFiles.RepoRoot` idiom that already exists for precisely this reason (GameFiles.cs:35-38, "The repo root: the parent of res://, which is where /data sits"), and accept that this creates a new desync surface, because the catalogue is deliberately not hashed (World.cs:283-284) and two clients loading different YAML would agree on every hash while playing different games. Until that lands, every stat change is a synchronised edit in two places and the tickets below say so.

## 3. Per area: what works, what is missing, what is defective

Severities are calibrated concretely, not qualitatively. CRITICAL means it causes wrong behaviour or lost player property in a normal match today. MAJOR means it causes a wrong or misleading result a player will notice, or it silently voids a test. MINOR means it is latent, cosmetic, or a coherence problem with no current trigger. Confidence is CERTAIN only where the claim was proven by executing code or by reading the exact line.

### 3.1 Power

**Works (do not rebuild).** The per-entity fields and their hashing (World.cs:81-82, :1811). Both serializers (World.Serialization.cs:144, :165). The /data wiring since BD-06 (DataLoader.cs:165-166, schema.structure.json:22-23) and the compiled mirror the selftest proves equal (World.cs:367-383). The production rate curve (World.cs:1646-1648), proven at 75 and 150 ticks. The superweapon charge gate (World.cs:1622), the veil gate (World.cs:1034) and the depot gate (World.cs:1604). Per-player tallies that correctly exclude the dead and the neutral (World.cs:1013-1019, :1567-1575). The AI's own tally and its 40-point plant margin (SkirmishAI.cs:74-75, :94-97). The client's power bar with the 75 per cent threshold in integer maths (Sidebar.cs:300-327), which is BD-10 done well.

**Missing.** GDD line 48 is the entire power spec in one sentence: "Each structure lists draw; total supply vs draw shown as a bar. Below 100%: production speed scales down linearly to 50%, radar goes dark, defensive turrets go offline at <75%. Deliberate 'sell power to sneak a superweapon' plays should be possible." Scored against it, the game is two clauses for five. The bar is done. The linear scaling is done. Radar going dark is not implemented and there is no radar to darken. Turrets going offline below 75 per cent is not implemented. And the sell-to-sneak play is not merely absent, it is inverted, because World.cs:1622 pauses the charge the instant supply drops below draw, so selling your plants to fund a superweapon stops the superweapon. GDD line 86 mandates the radar a second time, in the UI spec: "Radar minimap top-right above sidebar (the classic silhouette)." GDD line 85's low-power alert is also missing and is already filed as TICKET-P5-ALERT-02 (docs/tickets/P5-ALERT-and-balance-findings.md:45); it is not re-filed here, it is scheduled.

**Defective.**

- **PWR-D1. Turrets fire at zero power. CRITICAL, CERTAIN (proven by execution).** CombatSystem, World.cs:1129-1318, contains no power reference; a probe's unpowered turret and powered turret each dealt exactly 200 damage. Consequence: plant-sniping, which doc 00 line 24 names as a pillar and which the plant's 150 hp is balanced around, buys the attacker a production slowdown and nothing else. The defence line is untouched.
- **PWR-D2. Power is decorative in the economy that gets played. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** Opening base draws 40 against 100 supply, headroom 60. Whole catalogue is 250. Verified from the shipped YAML.
- **PWR-D3. The refinery's draw of 0 is a documented shipped value, and that is the finding. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** com_refinery.yaml:14-16 says so verbatim and points at TICKET-P5-BD-07, which doc 22 priced at line 1380 and which has been blocked on a signature ever since. The defect is not the number; it is that the project has known and not decided. Note the TDD states no refinery draw anywhere, so BD-07's 40 is a Balance judgement, not a spec recovery.
- **PWR-D4. The sidebar promises a threshold the sim does not honour. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** Sidebar.cs:307 turns the bar red and pulses it at exactly the point GDD s5 says the turrets die, and the turrets do not die. The comment at Sidebar.cs:295-296 says so out loud. The client is telling the truth about the design and lying about the game. Fixed for free by fixing PWR-D1, which is an argument for doing them together.
- **PWR-D5. The GDD's one named power play is inverted. Referred, not filed.** Behaviour certain; the reading of intent is a Game Designer call and this document declines to rate it as a defect. Note the shape of the problem: the sim has invented a third power semantic, a hard cliff at 100 per cent, and applied it to three systems the GDD never mentions, while the only two thresholds the GDD names are 100 and 75. Whether that is right is a decision, and it should be made rather than inherited.
- **PWR-D6. Neither tally bounds PlayerId above. MINOR, CERTAIN, no known trigger.** World.cs:1019 and :1573 guard `>= 0` and then index a `stackalloc int[_players]`. Silent stack corruption inside the deterministic sim is the worst possible place for a latent bug; one guard closes it.
- **PWR-D7. dir_turret.yaml's note is overstated. MINOR, CERTAIN.** It says the turret's power_draw "buys nothing back today". The 20 draw does contribute to the aggregate that gates production, the superweapon, the veil and the depot. What is dead is the turret-specific consequence. As written it invites a future reader to conclude the field is unread and safe to zero.
- **PWR-D8. Structure repair ignores power while the depot does not. MINOR, CERTAIN, arguably not a defect.** A base at zero supply cannot mend a tank but can mend the building the tank is parked beside. The GDD commits to neither. Flagged because it reads as a bug in a play-test.

### 3.2 Production buildings and the tech tree

**Works (do not rebuild).** `_queues` keyed per producer id (World.cs:317), so N independent lines already exist in the sim. ProductionSystem driving Factory and Construction Yard alike (World.cs:1641). Pay-as-you-build (World.cs:1656-1663) and the exact pro-rata refund it buys (World.cs:718-730). The ready-slot flow and the placement resync (World.cs:775-795). The /data catalogue bridge (DataLoader.cs:214-241) and the selftest that proves the nine building files reproduce the compiled defs exactly, including the completeness loop and the check that the reserved gate type stays undefined (Program.cs:1636-1665). The sidebar's gating philosophy: disallowed items are absent, not greyed, "progression should read as the tree growing" (Sidebar.cs:129-131). Doc 22's BD-17 (line 1459) is a good ticket and the design below extends it rather than competing with it.

**Missing.** GDD line 45: "Two parallel building queues (structures / defences) and two unit queues (infantry / vehicles) per production-structure type, [trademark elided] multi-queue with per-structure rally points." Two unit queues per production-structure type is meaningless with exactly one unit-producing structure type. Note honestly what this document does and does not discharge: the barracks split below delivers one queue per producer, which is what already ships. After all of Wave 6 lands, GDD line 45's four-queue promise is still unimplemented. That is a gap between the cited authority and the delivered thing and it is stated here rather than buried. GDD line 47: "Both factions can build replacement MCVs at the Factory once a Tech Centre exists." No Tech Centre exists anywhere. GDD line 55: "Air: Limited-count strike aircraft (airfield-slot model) plus a transport helicopter each. Air is a scalpel, not an army." GDD line 86: "Sidebar always visible on the right; tabbed (Buildings / Defence / Infantry / Vehicles / Aircraft)." The shipped sidebar is two flat arrays under two flat headers.

**Defective.**

- **PROD-D1. `Produce` has no producer notion. MAJOR, HIGH.** World.cs:843-853. This is Luke's question restated as a defect.
- **PROD-D2. Prerequisites are parsed and dropped. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** DataLoader.cs:129 and :170 populate; DataLoader.cs:194 and :251 discard. Precisely: this is true of units, where all twelve files author the key. It is not true of buildings, where `grep -rn "prerequisites:" data/buildings/` returns nothing, so on the structure side the field is inert end to end rather than dropped.
- **PROD-D3. The Veil Projector is unbuildable by any human player. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** Sidebar.cs:18-31 has seven structures and it is not among them. See section 1. Any ticket that lists it under a tab is describing a relocation of a button that does not exist.
- **PROD-D4. `AnchorOf` is arithmetically wrong for footprints 3 and 4. MAJOR, CERTAIN, latent.** World.cs:418 is `Map.CellOf(centre) - (FootprintOf(structType) - 1)`. For footprint 3 the centre is anchor+1.5, `CellOf` gives anchor+1, minus 2 gives anchor-1. For footprint 4 the centre is anchor+2.0, `CellOf` gives anchor+2, minus 3 gives anchor-1. schema.structure.json:25 permits footprint up to 4, so a YAML author can legally write a 3 today and the structure unblocks the wrong cells on death and on sale. Unreachable only because all nine files say 1 or 2. ADR-005 line 76 states the stakes: "A footprint mismatch is silent and fatal."
- **PROD-D5. The state hash covers factory queues but not Construction Yard queues. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** World.cs:1819. Already doc 22's DEF-16. The sharpest case is not the one the audits first reached for: BuildProgress and BuildPaid are hashed, so a head divergence normally surfaces within a tick, but `DefaultStructureType(2)` and `(3)` are both Cost 2000 and BuildTicks 300 (World.cs:370-371), so a factory-versus-refinery divergence at the yard's queue head produces identical BuildProgress and BuildPaid every tick and is undetectable until ReadyStructure is written at completion. Any new producer kind inherits the hole.
- **PROD-D6. The client can only reach one factory. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** `FindOwnStructure` (SkirmishLive.cs:536-541) returns the first match in view order, `_factoryId` is rebuilt every frame (SkirmishLive.cs:1125) and `QueueUnit` sends to it unconditionally (SkirmishLive.cs:639-641). A second factory is inert scenery despite the sim supporting N lines. com_factory.yaml's own note contradicts the client on exactly this point.
- **PROD-D7. The service depot shipped without its MapLoader arm. MAJOR, CERTAIN, latent.** MapLoader.cs:164-176 switches on eight kinds and ends `_ => throw new FormatException(...)`; `grep -n "ServiceDepot" sim/Ferrostorm.Sim/MapLoader.cs` returns nothing. Any .fmap carrying `structure P 8 x y` throws. Latent only because no committed map uses type 8. This is worth reading twice, because it is the exact trap every "add a building" ticket warns about, and the evidence that it has already been sprung once was lying on the floor.
- **PROD-D8. Two hard-coded `t <= 9` loop bounds gate every new struct type. MAJOR, CERTAIN.** World.cs:389 seeds only types 1 to 9, so a `DefaultStructureType(11)` arm is never seeded, `GetStructureType(11)` returns default with Cost 0, and Cost 0 is exactly what every command handler tests to refuse a type: the new building is unbuildable with no error. Program.cs:1653 is the second loop, implementing "Every compiled type must be authored". Leave it at 9 and the guarantee silently stops covering anything new. Raise it naively to 13 and the gate FAILS, because type 10 is ADR-005's reserved gate, has no def and no file. Both loops must skip `World.GateStructType` explicitly.
- **PROD-D9. Unit type 12 has no round-trip proof. MINOR, CERTAIN.** The unit half of the BD-06 check is hand-enumerated and never loads dir_vanguard_car.yaml. Compare the structure half, which walks the directory. A new unit YAML is silently unverified.
- **PROD-D10. The prerequisites data is internally inconsistent. MINOR, CERTAIN.** Four vehicles claim they need no factory (dir_cannon_tank.yaml:17, sod_shade_raider.yaml:18, dir_vanguard_car.yaml:19, dir_sentinel_scout.yaml:16 all read `[]`) while their siblings say `[com_factory]`. Further proof nothing reads it.
- **PROD-D11. campaign.txt's header is already stale. MINOR, CERTAIN.** It lists "1 power, 2 factory, 3 refinery, 5 turret, 6 superweapon, 7 veil, 8 depot" and omits 4 (construction yard) and 9 (wall). Struct 9 is a live sidebar button gated through `MatchConfig.AllowedStructures` like every other, so mission-01's `1,3,2,5` silently hides the WALL button. Correct behaviour, undocumented, and the header is the only place an author would look.

### 3.3 Unit placement and spawning

**Works (do not rebuild).** SeparationSystem (World.cs:1410-1552): spatial buckets rebuilt per tick, a bounded 3x3 neighbour scan, a 0.6-cell minimum, a leaky stall accumulator and an arrival-contagion rule, all deterministic and all debugged through three named failure modes. Ten units sent to one far rally point settled into ten distinct positions with nobody frozen. The 4-second stall drop at World.cs:1547 never fired once across four crowd harnesses; it is dormant on this path, not a hazard. Flow-field pathing (Map.cs:32-122): deterministic Dijkstra, tie-broken on cell index, no corner cutting, cached and correctly invalidated. Building placement validation and the ghost's agreement with the sim on geometry. Rally attribution through `GameEvent.C` (World.cs:1692), which is BD-14's fix and is correct.

**Missing.** No spawn-cell occupancy test. No sim-side rally at all, and Q004 already recommends taking SetRally into the wire format and is undecided. No exit move: a produced unit is created with `TargetX = x, TargetY = y` and Moving false, so without a client rally it stands in the doorway forever. No formations, confirmed as a gap at docs/design/21-ue5-portability-audit.md:31 and :67 (P4-PORT-05), conceded in code at World.cs:973 and in the backlog. Grouped units do not move as a blob, which is the more interesting answer: ten units ordered to one point arrived as a single-file diagonal chain roughly eight cells long, because the first arrival stops at the crowd-arrival radius and every follower contagion-settles behind it. That reads as the army refusing the order.

**Defective.**

- **SPAWN-D1. Units spawn perfectly stacked and stay stacked forever. CRITICAL, CERTAIN (reproduced independently by two agents to the decimal).** World.cs:1682-1694 plus the `!e.Moving` gate at World.cs:1433. Ten units at one position, minimum pairwise distance 0.0000. Harvesters do the same, and worse, because the auto-resume re-issues Harvest per harvester and they then travel as one indistinguishable object. This is almost certainly what Luke meant.
- **SPAWN-D2. A completed unit is deleted with no refund and no event if the ring is full. CRITICAL, CERTAIN.** World.cs:1680-1694. Measured: entities 2 to 2, credits 10000 to 9800, queue 0, live units 0. Two hundred credits and a rifle squad ceased to exist silently. ## 4. The design

### 4.1 Numbering, which is where a cheaper model will collide

ADR-005 is ratified and its reservation is absolute (ADR-005:72-76): "type 9 is the wall segment, type 10 is the gate" (reserved, unimplemented). DataLoader.cs already ships `"com_wall" => 9` and World.cs:406-407 already carries `public const int GateStructType = 10;` with FootprintOf special-casing it before the catalogue lookup, precisely so the reservation cannot be silently lost. **Number from 11 upward. Do not take 10.**

Doc 22's numbers are stale and must not be followed literally. BD-17 clause 2 (doc 22:1471) says `com_radar_uplink=9`, which collides with the ratified wall. Doc 22 section 4.4's table (Wall 11/9, Emplacement 12/10, Bastion 13/11, Gate reserved/12, Radar 14/13, Outpost 15/14) is not what got ratified either. Doc 22 line 2062 diagnoses the disease correctly and then prescribes a stale cure. Doc 22 is a WIP file and is not this document's to edit; the fix belongs in a new ADR that supersedes both.

**Struct types.** 1-8 as shipped. 9 com_wall (shipped). 10 gate (RESERVED by ADR-005, do not take). 11 com_barracks (new). 12 com_radar_uplink (new, BD-09 moved off its stale 9). 13 com_airfield (reserved now, implemented with the aircraft). 14 com_emplacement (reserved for doc 22 DEF-12). 15 dir_bastion (reserved for DEF-13b). 16 com_outpost (reserved for P5-ECON-14).

**EntityKind.** The enum is a save-format byte under an APPEND ONLY comment and currently ends `ServiceDepot = 10, Wall = 11`, so the next free value is 12. Append: `Barracks = 12, RadarUplink = 13, Airfield = 14, Emplacement = 15, Bastion = 16, Outpost = 17`.

**Other enums, verified rather than assumed.** `GameEventType` ends `Captured = 11` (World.cs:159-172), so the next free event value is **12**, not 8. An implementer working from a claim of "seven members" would pick 8 and collide with SuperweaponReady. `CommandType` ends `LaunchSuper = 15` (World.cs:20), so `SetRally = 16` is safe; the enum has an unused hole at 1, do not reuse it. Unit types are dense 1 through 12, so a repair vehicle is unit type 13; ADR-005 governs struct types only and does not constrain unit types, which is a real argument for building a repair vehicle as a unit rather than as a building.

**Footprint.** Every new building is `footprint: 2` in its YAML and no code change is needed, because `FootprintOf` is data-driven (World.cs:413 reads `GetStructureType(structType).Footprint`) and the gate is the only hardcoded case, for the reason the comment at World.cs:406 gives. Do NOT add a case to FootprintOf for a new building: that is hand-editing a stat in code and CLAUDE.md forbids it. Do NOT give the airfield a 3x3 pad, however tempting, until PROD-D4 is fixed. Ticket P5-BASE-01 fixes it, and once it lands the footprint-2 constraint lifts at zero cost.

**The reservation's real enforcement surface.** Recording the numbers is not enough, because two loops enumerate the range and neither knows about the reservation: World.cs:389 and Program.cs:1653, both `for (int t = 1; t <= 9; t++)`. Both must move with any new type and both must skip `World.GateStructType`. The ADR must state the loop bounds and the gate-skip as part of the reservation, not just the numbers. This is exactly where ADR-005's reservation actually bites and it is where doc 22's own BD-09 fell over.

### 4.2 Power

**The tally, first and hash-neutral.** Factor the two identical sim loops (World.cs:1013-1019 in DetectionSystem, World.cs:1567-1575 in ProductionSystem) into one private `ComputePower(Span<int> supply, Span<int> draw)` with the bounded guard `e.PlayerId < 0 || e.PlayerId >= _players`, closing PWR-D6. Add `private static bool AtLeast75(int supply, int draw) => draw <= 0 || supply * 4 >= draw * 3;` beside it, in integer maths with no division, which is the same expression Sidebar.cs:307 already uses, so PWR-D4's honesty becomes structural rather than coincidental.

Two things about this refactor are load-bearing and both were nearly got wrong. First, the reach is TWO sites, not four: SkirmishAI.cs:74-75 is two lines fused inside a large entity-classification switch that accumulates scalars scoped to one player, and SkirmishLive.cs:1089 is client-side and also scalar. Neither can call the proposed signature without restructuring, and neither should. Second, and this is the trap: **do not compute power once per Step and pass it to every system.** CombatSystem can destroy a power plant on the tick ProductionSystem then reads, and the shipped behaviour is that ProductionSystem sees the post-combat total. Collapsing to one call moves hashes. Keep the calls separate and per-system.

**The turret gate.** CombatSystem has no tally today, so the turret guard needs a THIRD call site that the refactor must add. This is not a detail: a design that says "call it from DetectionSystem and ProductionSystem" and then writes `supply[e.PlayerId]` inside CombatSystem does not compile, and the most likely repair a cheaper model reaches for is the one thing forbidden above.

The guard goes immediately after the real CombatSystem guard, which is `if (!e.Alive || e.WeaponId == 0) continue;` at **World.cs:1141**. It is not at World.cs:957; that line is inside MovementSystem, whose guard reads `if (!e.Alive || !e.Moving || e.Speed == Fix64.Zero) continue;`. Doc 22's DEF-10 points at the wrong system and so did the audit that repeated it. The guard is:

```
// GDD s5 line 48: a browned-out base cannot power its guns. The continue sits
// ABOVE the cooldown decrement deliberately: a dead turret does not reload.
if (e.Kind == EntityKind.Turret && !AtLeast75(supply[e.PlayerId], draw[e.PlayerId])) { _entities[i] = e; continue; }
```

Use the divisionless form, not doc 22's `100 * supply[p] / draw[p]`, which truncates. Do not generalise to all armed structures: the GDD says defensive turrets, and DEF-12's and DEF-13b's emplacements join the guard by kind as they land.

**Snapshot semantics must be pinned in the ADR.** A tally computed inside CombatSystem necessarily sees pre-combat power, so a plant destroyed on tick N disables its turrets on tick N+1, whereas ProductionSystem already sees the post-combat total and slows production on tick N. Both are defensible, both are deterministic, and they produce different hashes. Choose one and write it down. The recommendation is pre-combat, because it is what the natural placement gives and because a turret that dies on the same tick as its plant is harder to read than one that dies a frame later.

**Which scenarios break, and one that rots without breaking.** This is the part a cheaper model will skip and it is the part that matters. `grep -rn "SpawnTurret" sim/ game/` returns five call sites, not two.

- **ScenarioVeil (Program.cs:955).** A turret for player 1 while the only plant, line 956, belongs to player 0. Player 1's turret goes offline, the baseline rifle is never engaged, and the scenario throws. CI goes red, which is the good failure. Fix by adding a plant for player 1 clear of the turret and the projector.
- **ScenarioStealth (Program.cs:575).** The turret is player 0's and runs for 100 ticks before the plant arrives at line 586. The "undetected raider was shot" assertion keeps PASSING for the wrong reason, the turret being dead rather than blind. Fix by spawning a plant above line 575 and keeping the line 586 plant for Rule 2's geometry.
- **THE `walls` GATE, PHASE G (Program.cs:1415-1416). READ THIS ONE TWICE.** `worldG.SpawnWall(1, 30, 28+k)` five times, then `worldG.SpawnTurret(1, 32, 29)`. Player 1 owns five walls (draw 0) and one turret (draw 20) and no plant at all: supply 0 against draw 20, exactly 0 per cent power. The turret gate kills that turret for the whole phase. The assertion at Program.cs:1440 is that the howitzer's hp is unchanged, and the gate prints it verbatim: "a howitzer at range 8 dealt 60 direct and exactly 30 to each orthogonal neighbour, breached the line, and took nothing back from the turret". The comment at Program.cs:1408-1411 calls this "the assertion that keeps turtling beatable" and ties it to GDD s6 line 53. After the gate lands it proves nothing, because the turret takes nothing back from the howitzer on account of being dead, not on account of being out-ranged. **And the golden hash will not move**, because the turret is 10.5 cells from the gun, never acquires, never fires, and its Cooldown stays 0, so the early `continue` produces a bit-identical entity. The golden check cannot catch this. Only a human reading PHASE G can. This is exactly how a gate rots: it keeps passing, it keeps printing a sentence that is no longer true, and the property it was written to protect quietly stops being protected. Fix by spawning a plant for player 1 in worldG.
- **Program.cs:1736 (the bench rig)** alternates turrets and plants per player, so it is probably fine; verify rather than assume. **Program.cs:2190 (StDebug)** is a debug entry point, not a golden scenario.
- **MapLoader.cs:171** is a fifth turret source that no scenario audit reaches. The four shipped .fmap files carry no structures, so nothing breaks today, but the path is live.

Therefore the acceptance criterion for the turret gate is NOT "diff the hashes". It is: **every SpawnTurret call site is re-read by a human and its owner's supply against draw recorded per tick range, hash movement or not.**

**Honest draws.** BD-07's numbers, rebased: com_construction_yard 20 (was 0), com_refinery 40 (was 0), dir_superweapon 150 (was 100). Unchanged: factory 40, turret 20, depot 30, veil projector 60, plant supply 100 cost 300, wall 0. Resulting curve: opening base 100 draw against a 100-supply plant, so the first plant is mandatory; two refineries plus four turrets is 220, three plants; plus the superweapon 370, four plants. That turns a 300-credit line item into a roughly 1200-credit commitment and it is what gives the turret gate teeth. Note honestly what the opening base then is: **exactly supply 100 equals draw 100**, which sits on the inclusive boundary of the veil, depot and superweapon gates and of AtLeast75 simultaneously. Any single further draw tips a fresh base into brown-out before its second plant finishes. That is a zero-margin design and it is a Balance decision, not an accident to be discovered in a play-test.

The AI is safe under these numbers, traced by hand rather than assumed: the ladder is `!hasPlant ? 1 : refinery < 0 ? 3 : factory < 0 ? 2 : supply < draw + 40 ? 1 : ...`, so at CY 20 plus refinery 40 plus factory 40 it reaches supply 100 against draw 100, `100 < 140` fires, a second plant is queued, and it settles at 400 against 270 with a turret and a superweapon. It does not brown-out lock.

**The superweapon scenario's arithmetic, which the audit got wrong and which cannot be fixed the way it says.** ScenarioSuperweapon (Program.cs:812-840) spawns a Construction Yard at line 814, two plants at 815-816 for supply 200, and a superweapon at 818 with draw 100. Selling plant1 lands supply 100 against draw 100 and the inclusive `>=` at World.cs:1622 keeps the charge running. **That equality IS the test**: the comment at Program.cs:833 says "supply 200 -> 100, draw 100: boundary holds" and the assertion at Program.cs:840 fires if it does not. Under the new draws the superweapon is 150 AND the yard is 20, so draw becomes **170, not 150**. The audit reasoned about the superweapon it was raising and never counted the yard whose draw it was also raising. Selling plant1 then gives 100 against 170, the charge pauses, and Program.cs:840 throws.

And the obvious repair is forbidden by the rule the design itself states, "only power-arithmetic constants may change". Plants supply 100 each, so supply can only ever be 100, 200 or 300 and can never equal 170; the boundary-equality test cannot be preserved by recomputing any constant. **The escape exists and must be named or a cheaper model will loosen the assertion**: `SpawnPowerPlant` carries a nullable supply override (World.cs:455-458, `int sup = supply ?? def.PowerSupply`), so `SpawnPowerPlant(0, 10, 6, supply: 70)` plus plant2 reaches exactly 170 and the boundary test survives intact.

The rest of the runner's power arithmetic must be re-derived rather than forecast. Two scenarios are provably immune and their hashes must NOT move: ScenarioProduction (Program.cs:193-195) spawns only a plant and a factory, no yard and no refinery, so the 75 and 150 tick assertions survive untouched; ScenarioDepot (Program.cs:1212-1214) is a plant plus a depot, same. Enumerate which scenarios own a Construction Yard or a refinery before regenerating anything. That is a five-minute grep, not a forecast, and "expect most of the 24 to move" is exactly the kind of instruction that gets a golden diff rubber-stamped.

**The radar.** Struct type 12, `EntityKind.RadarUplink = 13`. Cost 900, build 150 ticks, hp 1000, draw 80, sight 10, footprint 2, prerequisites [com_factory]. The five code sites doc 22's BD-09 misses and that silently break things: `StructureCatalogue.TypeIdOf` (DataLoader.cs:214) throws `FormatException` on an unknown id the moment the YAML lands in the directory the selftest walks; `KindOf` (DataLoader.cs:229) likewise; `SeedStructureTypes` (World.cs:389); the selftest authorship loop (Program.cs:1653); and MapLoader.cs:164-176's switch. Add `EntityKind.RadarUplink` to `IsStructure` (World.cs:860-863): omitting it silently breaks sell, repair, capture, rubble-unblock, placement adjacency AND the VictorySystem short-game rule, with no compile error.

**And the blackout is a bigger decision than it looks.** No shipped map spawns any structure, so no map spawns a radar, and the player's opening base is an MCV and a Construction Yard. The day the blackout lands, every skirmish and all three campaign missions begin with a permanently blank minimap reading RADAR OFFLINE until the player builds a 900-credit building that exists in no map, no mission script and no opening loadout. That may well be correct genre behaviour, but it is a Game Designer decision about the opening experience, it needs the four .fmap files and the mission scripts revisited, and it must not be smuggled inside an "add a building" ticket. Note also that a client-side blackout binds only the human: SkirmishAI reads `w.Entities` directly with no fog or visibility filter, so the AI cannot be blinded. That is consistent with the existing stance, since the AI already ignores fog entirely, but a GDD s5 rule that applies to one side is a decision and should be recorded as one.

Minimap pings still render while blanked, deliberately: blanking the base-under-attack ping would make a blackout a stealth nerf to the alert system.

### 4.3 Production buildings and the tech tree

**The producer link.** A new required key on the unit schema, because `role` cannot carry this: the shipped roles are twelve values for twelve units and `ToTypeDef` already overloads "economy" to mean `EntityKind.Harvester`, so overloading it again would be a second source of truth.

```
"produced_at": { "type": "string", "pattern": "^(dir|sod|com)_[a-z0-9_]+$",
  "description": "Structure id of the building that produces this unit. The producer IS a prerequisite; do not repeat it in the prerequisites list." }
```

Every data/units/*.yaml gains one line: com_rifle_squad, com_rocket_squad and com_engineer to com_barracks; everything else to com_factory. The engineer's placement at the barracks is a **decision, not a reading**. GDD line 60 is "Shared: Engineer (capture), Harvester, MCV, Transport helicopter." That is a shared roster line, not an infantry line, and it groups the engineer with the harvester and the MCV, both of which go to the factory. The genre idiom builds engineers at the infantry building and that is the recommendation, but it belongs in the docs/questions/ entry with the Tech Centre call, not asserted as textual support it does not have.

**Prerequisites, cleaned so the producer is never repeated** (this resolves PROD-D10): com_rifle_squad, com_rocket_squad, com_engineer, dir_sentinel_scout, dir_vanguard_car, dir_cannon_tank and sod_shade_raider all `[]`; com_harvester `[com_refinery]` unchanged; dir_howitzer, dir_bulwark_tank and sod_phantom_tank `[com_service_depot]`; com_mcv `[com_radar_uplink]`.

That last one is a decision and it needs the Game Designer. GDD line 47 says the MCV needs a Tech Centre, which does not exist. com_mcv.yaml:17 says `[com_factory]`, which under `produced_at` becomes a tautology and therefore says nothing. Doc 22's BD-17 clause 7 already ordered a docs/questions/ entry with an owner and a decide-by date and named the candidate resolution: the Radar Uplink absorbs the Tech Centre role. Take that candidate, file the question anyway, and do not let a cheaper model silently pick it. Tier-2 vehicles moving behind the Service Depot is likewise a design call: it is the cheapest way to give the depot's 1200 credits a reason to exist in a build order, and it matches the tiers already authored in /data.

**Structure prerequisite tree**, in data/buildings/*.yaml (note the key is currently authored in none of the nine files, so this is new authoring rather than an edit): com_power_plant `[]`; com_refinery, com_barracks, dir_turret and sod_veil_projector `[com_power_plant]`; com_factory `[com_refinery]`; com_service_depot and com_radar_uplink `[com_factory]`; dir_superweapon and com_airfield `[com_radar_uplink]`; com_wall `[]` (never queued, ADR-005 clause 3); com_construction_yard `[]` (MCV-deployed, BuildTicks 0 keeps it out). The existing hard faction check at World.cs:804 stays; it is orthogonal.

**com_barracks**: faction common, cost 500, build_time_ticks 100, hp 800, power_supply 0, power_draw 20, sight_range 5, footprint 2, weapon_ids [], prerequisites [com_power_plant]. Cheap and early, because that is what makes an infantry rush a real strategy rather than a factory afterthought. Balance co-sign required.

**Sim changes.** `UnitTypeDef` gains two trailing defaulted parameters, `int[] Prereqs = null` and `int ProducedAt = 2`; `StructureTypeDef` gains `int[] Prereqs = null`. Trailing and defaulted so the twelve compiled entries and the nine default arms keep compiling. `HasPrereqs(int player, int[] ids)` scans `_entities` in index order for a living entity with the right player and StructType; index order is deterministic and StructType is verifiably set on all nine spawns (World.cs:266, 464, 479, 494, 513, 528, 542, 562, 589). `Produce` gains, after the faction check and using `break` not `return` so the shared `_entities[c.EntityId] = e;` epilogue at World.cs:857 still runs: `if (!IsProducer(e.Kind)) break;` replacing the Factory test, then `if (e.StructType != def.ProducedAt) break;` (this single line is the barracks/factory split), then `if (!HasPrereqs(c.PlayerId, def.Prereqs)) break;`.

`IsProducer` must then be used in four places, of which three fail silently if missed: `Produce`; ProductionSystem at World.cs:1641, **miss this and the barracks queue never advances with no error**; `CancelProduce` at World.cs:723, miss it and barracks orders are uncancellable; and the state hash at World.cs:1819, which simultaneously fixes PROD-D5 and must land in the same regeneration. `IsStructure` (World.cs:860-863) gains Barracks, RadarUplink and Airfield; they are real buildings that block, sell for half, repair, count for the victory test and are capturable, but they are not barriers, so `IsBarrier` is untouched. `SpawnBarracks` copies `SpawnFactory`'s shape; add the PlaceStructure arm (World.cs:785-795) and **the MapLoader arm (MapLoader.cs:164-176)**, which the Service Depot is already missing (PROD-D7). `Ralliable` in the client gains Barracks and Airfield: rally points are per-structure by BD-14 and infantry want them most.

### 4.4 The airfield, and why it must wait

A queue is a pipeline: you pay over time, the unit pops out, the queue advances, and you repeat without limit, bounded only by credits. A slot model is a lease: the airfield owns N slots (propose 2), buying an aircraft occupies a slot for the LIFE of the aircraft rather than for the duration of its build, and the slot frees only when that aircraft dies. Your air force is therefore capped at airfields times N, permanently, no matter how rich you are, and between sorties the aircraft returns to its pad to rearm and is unavailable. That is precisely what GDD line 55 means by "Air is a scalpel, not an army": you get two strike jets and you must choose their target, you cannot mass thirty of them like tanks. It is a cap mechanic dressed as a building.

An airfield cannot land without aircraft. It would be a 1000-credit building with a 50-power draw that produces nothing, occupies four cells and can only be sold. There is no partial version, and the barracks work will make it look like a twenty-line copy of SpawnFactory. Do not ship it. P4-PORT-02 (docs/design/21-ue5-portability-audit.md:56-58) already says the air layer is big and needs an Architect ADR before any code, and there is no altitude concept, no flying EntityKind and no anti-air flag in any weapon. Reserve struct type 13 and `EntityKind.Airfield = 14` now anyway, because reserving is free and collision is silent and fatal, and record the loop bounds with them per section 4.1.

For the eventual ADR to price honestly: slots need a home-airfield link per aircraft, which is either a new `Entity` field (hashing every entity in the game) or a derived count that cannot express which pad to land on. Recommend the field, accept the regeneration, and do it inside the aircraft ADR rather than smuggling it in early.

### 4.5 Unit placement and spawning

**The order is the whole design, and the audit had it backwards.** Rally goes into the sim FIRST. Then, and only then, the spawn-cell occupancy test.

Here is why, measured rather than argued. A produced unit gets no exit move and stands in the doorway forever. `SpawnOffsets` has exactly eleven entries (World.cs:1557-1558). Add an occupancy test while units still never leave and the ring saturates permanently: with the proposed fix applied to a copy of the sim and sixteen rifle squads queued at one factory with no rally, which is the default player experience and precisely Luke's play-test, the measured result was eleven spawned and then the factory frozen at full BuildProgress for the remaining four hundred simulated seconds. Five paid units never appeared and the factory never produced again. The occupancy test converts "units stack invisibly", which is ugly but production continues, into "production permanently dead", which is fatal. The audit called that fix "highest feel-per-work in the whole audit"; it is the most dangerous item in the set if it lands first.

**And the hold-at-100-per-cent restructure as the audit wrote it drains the entire treasury.** This is the single most important trap in this document. The sketch is `if (chosen < 0) { e.BuildProgress = total; _entities[i] = e; continue; }`. But World.cs:1667-1668 zero BuildProgress **and BuildPaid** at the top of the completion block, ABOVE the isCy branch and above anything the sketch touches. So every held tick re-enters with `BuildPaid = 0`, and World.cs:1660 recomputes `owed = defCost * total / total - 0`, the FULL unit cost, every tick. Measured on a patched copy: credits 40000 to 0, the entire treasury, for eleven units worth 2200. At 15 ticks per second that is **3000 credits per second, silent and permanent**. The audit sensed the hazard and wrote "Note BuildPaid must NOT be zeroed while holding", then misdiagnosed the consequence as a refund bug and never located the cause, which is two lines it left untouched. **Do not implement the hold without moving the zeroing at World.cs:1667-1668 below the hold check**, and add a runner assertion that a blocked factory's credit drain over N held ticks is exactly zero. That assertion is the test that catches this.

**Rally into the sim** (resolves Q004 resolution 2, which ADR-001 already implies since a rally point is gameplay). `CommandType.SetRally = 16`, appended after LaunchSuper = 15, never renumbered. `Entity` gains `Fix64 RallyX, RallyY; bool HasRally;` appended to the struct. `ApplyCommandCore` case SetRally rejects anything that is not a Factory or Construction Yard, clamps X and Y exactly as the Move case does, and treats `AuxId == -1` as a clear. Append the three fields to `ComputeStateHash` after FieldCloaked and to both serializers in the same order; `StateHash.Add(bool)` and `Add(Fix64)` both exist (Determinism.cs:74-75). In ProductionSystem after the spawn, if the producing factory HasRally, set the new unit's TargetX/TargetY/Moving/UseFlow directly rather than synthesising a Command: the sim does not queue its own commands anywhere and starting now is a bigger change than this needs. Harvesters honour the rally, matching the client's current auto-harvest precedent. No YAML: a rally is per-instance runtime state, not a stat, so nothing goes in /data and neither schema changes.

Client: delete `_rally` (SkirmishLive.cs:147) and the ProductionComplete rally block; the IssueOrder rally branch sends `SetRally` instead of writing the dictionary. Keep `_rallyMarkers` and ForgetRally, which is a node-lifetime concern, but drive the marker position from the sim's own RallyX/RallyY so the marker cannot drift. This also closes SPAWN-D9.

**Let the rally survive the crowd-arrival shortcut** (SPAWN-D3's other half). Add `bool Departing`, set true on a rally-spawn, cleared the tick the unit's cell differs from its spawn cell or when it reaches the target, and add `&& !e.Departing` to the World.cs:974-976 guard. Note the flag is for units only: harvesters are `EntityKind.Harvester` and the shortcut is gated on `e.Kind == EntityKind.Unit`, so they never reach it and need no Departing. Append it to the hash and both serializers.

**Then the occupancy test.** Add a mobile-occupancy check to the World.cs:1685 guard. Do not hand-roll the predicate: `ValidPlacement` (World.cs:910-916) and `ValidFoundation` (World.cs:882-889) already do this scan and both use the broader and more correct `!o.Alive || IsStructure(o.Kind)` rather than an explicit kind list. The explicit list `Unit or Harvester` happens to be complete today only because MCVs are `EntityKind.Unit` with UnitType 7 rather than a kind of their own, which is a coincidence that a future EntityKind append breaks silently. Reuse the existing predicate. The scan is O(n) once per unit built, not per tick, so it is negligible against the TDD s6 budget.

**Then the separation corrections.** Two, and only two. (a) Perturb `dy` on the same `i < j` sign convention that World.cs:1503 already uses for `dx`. **Do not replace the existing tiebreak with an index hash.** The proposed `int k = (i * 7 + j * 13) & 3;` is provably asymmetric: swapping i and j changes the key by 6(j-i), and 6 is congruent to 2 mod 4, so the two evaluations agree whenever (j-i) is even. Worked example: i=0, j=2 gives k = 26 & 3 = 2; i=2, j=0 gives k = 14 & 3 = 2. Identical k, so both units receive the identical push vector and translate together forever without ever separating, for half of all pairs. The shipped `i < j` predicate is already perfectly symmetric; the only defect is that dy is left at zero. (b) Clamp the total push to the unit's own speed before applying it, guarding against a zero-length divide, so a stack walks out instead of detonating.

**Formations** (P4-PORT-05) last. The client version, offsetting each unit's target off a sorted selection, moves no hashes and is visible polish; the sim version is a Formation command carrying the group and belongs with the netcode, exactly as Q004 recommends for rally.

**Two things the sim should not bend for.** The MCV deploy deadlock (SPAWN-D6) is mostly cured by the occupancy test; for the remaining hand-walked case the sim already has the right rule and the player simply cannot see it, so the fix is a client toast, not a sim change. And the ghost honesty fix (SPAWN-D7, SPAWN-D8) must NOT move the cap and credit tests into `ValidPlacement`: that function is the sim's geometry predicate and other callers depend on its current meaning. Keep authority in the command handler; the client is just being told to ask a fuller question. When it does, it must read `_world.GetStructureType(type).Cost`, the live catalogue, not `World.DefaultStructureType(type).Cost`.

### 4.6 Repair

Almost everything worth doing here is client-side and hash-neutral, and it closes the whole of Luke's complaint. Read the live flag as a post-Step read of `_world.Entities[id].Repairing`, which is an established pattern in this file, precedented by the rally code and stated at SkirmishLive.cs:1129. Do not add Repairing to `SnapshotInterpolator.ViewEntity`; it touches the Presentation assembly for no gain.

The headline fix is to partition the selection before acting. If there is at least one structure, send the Repair commands and play "ui_confirm". If there are zero structures and at least one mobile, do NOT play "ui_confirm"; toast that repair is for structures and to send vehicles to a Service Depot. If the selection is mixed, repair the structures and say how many units still need a depot. Moving `_audio.Play("ui_confirm", -8)` from SkirmishLive.cs:1890 inside the structures-found branch is the single line that kills the lie.

The feature Luke actually wants is R on a mobile-only selection sending those units to the depot: find the player's nearest live ServiceDepot to the selection centroid, issue PathMove to a point about 2 cells from the depot centre, comfortably inside the proven radius-4 aura, and enrol them in the existing "player-directed, do not auto-resume" set. Because the depot heals moving units, they start mending the instant they cross the boundary: no arrival logic, no parking, no state machine. This is exactly the P5-ECON-06/07 pattern already shipped for harvesters, including its no-refinery guard.

**But it must not reintroduce the lie one layer out.** The depot heals nothing during a base-wide brown-out (REP-D6) and nothing at zero credits (World.cs:1611). Send units to a depot under either condition and they drive over, park inside the aura, heal not at all, and the game has just confidently told the player it handled it. That is REP-D1's exact sin. Read supply against draw and credits BEFORE promising anything, and toast "DEPOT OFFLINE: LOW POWER" or "NO CREDITS TO REPAIR" instead. If no depot exists, say so and name the price. Note `FindOwnStructure` (SkirmishLive.cs:536) returns one structure, not the nearest of many, so this needs a new finder.

The rest is legibility: an amber pulsing ring on repairing structures sized off FootprintOf, using the rally gold already defined rather than a new colour; a selection readout that states the real rate ("REPAIRING 15 cr/s", because 2 hp per tick at 15 Hz is 30 hp/s for 15 cr/s) and shows the stall **per structure** rather than off a global credit test, since index-ordered charging (REP-D8) means some selected buildings genuinely are not being repaired while others are; structure health bars, widened off the `Mobile(hv.Kind)` gate, with a **width scale off FootprintOf** and not just a height bump, because the shipped bar is about 0.9 wide and tuned for mobiles and would float a third-width inside a construction yard's roof; a radius-4 depot aura ring, with `World.DepotRepairRadiusCells` exposed as a public const so the client and the sim cannot drift (hash-neutral, since naming a literal changes no behaviour, but note this one item does touch sim/World.cs and so the "part A is entirely client-side" claim is not quite true); a wall-run confirmation guard reusing the sell-guard shape, with the comment at SkirmishLive.cs:1895-1896 corrected, because 1200 credits per second is not "cheap"; multi-select repair changed from per-entity toggle to set-on; a hint bar that says "repair bldgs"; retiring the depot's dead rally affordance; and rank pips, which are the cheapest legibility win available and which make the third repair path learnable.

On the sim side there are exactly two things worth arguing for and neither is the repair vehicle. **The AI should repair and should build a depot**, because an AI whose base degrades permanently and whose army never heals is not the opponent the balance numbers assume, and it is the one sim-side repair item a play-test would notice. **Depot stacking (REP-D5) is a Game Designer call**, not an implementer's: decide whether five depots healing one tank at 10 hp per tick is a strategy or a bug, and write the answer down either way.

The **Repair Vehicle** is unit type 13 and the cheapest correct implementation reuses the depot loop wholesale, with a smaller radius and no power gate (a vehicle carries its own generator, which also sidesteps the PWR-D8 asymmetry). Do not invent a new EntityKind for it: a repair vehicle is a Unit with a UnitType, exactly as the engineer is (World.cs:1078 keys capture on UnitType 11). But two things must be said before anyone starts. First, it answers a sample-roster line, not a system statement, and it would not change the felt experience at all; if there is appetite for exactly one sim repair ticket, it is the AI, not the vehicle. Second, and this is section 2 restated as a design fault: authoring data/units/com_repair_vehicle.yaml and adding a sim branch **ships nothing**, because the client never loads /data and `GetUnitType(13)` returns default with Cost 0. It must also be added to the compiled `_unitTypes`, which is the hand-editing CLAUDE.md forbids. That contradiction is not the implementer's to resolve and the vehicle is blocked behind P5-DATA-01 for exactly that reason. Note in its favour, for whoever schedules it: the unit catalogue is explicitly not hashed (World.cs:283-284) and nothing enumerates it, so the catalogue entry plus the YAML plus the sidebar entry can land as a hash-neutral first commit with the sim branch second, which is the de-risking step the audit's blanket "B1 moves hashes" throws away.

Do not build a drive-in pad. Do not build a separate infantry-healing building; the depot already heals infantry and the GDD is silent. Do not reconcile the two power-gate philosophies (REP-D6 versus PWR-D8) in code: it moves hashes for no felt benefit, and documenting the asymmetry in com_service_depot.yaml's notes is free. Do rename one of the two Depots (REP-D11); a `name` change is free and the GDD edit is free, but the struct type number 8 must not move, and note that `TypeIdOf` keys on the id string (DataLoader.cs:214) so an `id` rename is a two-arm code edit as well as a YAML edit.

CLAUDE.md line 35: changing a golden hash is a replay-compatibility break requiring an ADR plus Architect sign-off. sim/golden-hashes.txt holds 24 hashes (27 lines, 3 of them comment). ADR-005 line 87's "all 23 existing golden hashes" is stale text and wants an amendment note.

## 7. What was dropped, and why

This section exists so that nobody re-invents a demolished proposal by reaching past this document into the raw audits. Every item below was written confidently and is wrong.

1. **"Hold the factory at 100 per cent when the ring is blocked", as sketched.** Drains 3000 credits per second, measured: 40000 credits for eleven units worth 2200, because World.cs:1667-1668 zero BuildPaid above the branch the fix touches and every held tick recomputes the full cost. The proposal's own note ("BuildPaid must NOT be zeroed while holding") sensed the hazard and never located the cause. See P5-SPAWN-04.
2. **"Land the occupancy test first; it is the highest feel-per-work item."** Measured: bricks the factory after exactly eleven units, because the eleven-offset ring saturates permanently while units still have no exit move. It is the most dangerous item in the set if it lands before rally. Order inverted in Wave 4.
3. **"GameEventType has seven members."** It has ELEVEN, up to `Captured = 11` (World.cs:159-172). An implementer told seven would pick 8 and collide with SuperweaponReady.
4. **"Insert the turret power guard after World.cs:957."** That is MovementSystem. The real CombatSystem guard is World.cs:1141. Doc 22's DEF-10 carries the same error and the hedge "verify the line number after the refactor moves it" misdirects: the line is wrong today, before any refactor.
5. **"Call ComputePower from DetectionSystem and ProductionSystem", then read `supply[]` in CombatSystem.** Does not compile. CombatSystem has no tally and the proposal never adds one. The likeliest repair a cheaper model reaches for is hoisting the tally into Step, which is the one thing the same document loudly forbids because it moves hashes. Relatedly, "the three sim call sites must stay three separate calls" describes a world that does not exist: there are two.
6. **"BD-07 makes the superweapon scenario's draw 150."** It makes it 170: the proposal raised the Construction Yard's draw and then forgot to count it. And its own rule ("only power-arithmetic constants may change") is unsatisfiable, because plants supply 100 each and supply can never equal 170. The `supply:` override at World.cs:455-458 is the escape and the proposal never names it.
7. **"Replace the separation tiebreak with `(i * 7 + j * 13) & 3`."** Provably asymmetric for every same-parity index pair, which is half of all pairs: those units get an identical push vector and never separate. The shipped `i < j` predicate is already correct; only dy was missing. Delegating the symmetry check to a human reviewer is not a design.
8. **"Add a case to FootprintOf for any new building that is not 2x2."** FootprintOf is data-driven (World.cs:413); the gate is its only hardcoded case and the comment at World.cs:406 says why. The instruction steers an implementer into hand-editing a stat in code, which CLAUDE.md forbids. Put `footprint` in the YAML.
9. **"Keep every new building at footprint 2 because AnchorOf is broken."** The premise is right and the conclusion is unnecessary: the correction is a hash-neutral one-liner (`- FootprintOf(t) / 2`) that is bit-identical for 1 and 2. It is ticket P5-BASE-01 and it lifts the constraint for free.
10. **"The AI will still build tanks and look almost normal."** It builds nothing. `lineHolds` is never true once types 2 and 3 are refused, so it produces only refused rifles and rockets forever and never attacks. The priority was right; the reasoning was backwards, and the prescribed edit sites (:184-193) are the selection switch rather than the routing at :196 and the guards at :145 and :165 that actually break.
11. **"Any rally inside the build radius of its own factory is dead."** The shortcut radius is 4 from the unit's own position; BuildRadius is 5 and CyBuildRadius is 7, so rallies at 5 to 7 cells work. Real defect, smaller blast radius.
12. **"A wall run costs 600 credits per second to repair."** 1200. The probe used 40 segments because the sell guard's comment says 40; the real cap is 80 (World.cs:427). Worse than reported.
13. **"A ServiceDepot whose owner's supply meets its draw."** The gate is base-wide, not depot-local (World.cs:1604). A fully-powered depot on a browned-out base heals nothing, proven. An implementer following the wording writes the wrong condition.
14. **"The Repair Vehicle is the only GDD-mandated gap."** GDD line 62 opens "**Directorate (sample):**" and lists eight other unbuilt things in the same breath. It is mandated exactly as much as the Commando. Contrast line 55 on air, which is a system statement.
15. **"D5: the sell-to-sneak play is a MAJOR defect."** The auditor's own hedge ("reading of intent is mine") is the honest part, and its own text concedes it is a Game Designer call. Referred as a question, not filed as a defect.
16. **"`grep -rn -i "depot|heal|hospital|mend|medic"` proves the GDD is silent."** That command returns nothing, because plain grep treats the alternation as a literal. The conclusion is right and the evidence was not run as written. Recorded because in a report whose method is proof by empty grep, a grep that could not have produced its quoted output is a methodology hit.
17. **"`grep -rni formation sim/` returns nothing."** It returns two hits, one of which the same report cites two sentences later. The substance (no formation implementation) stands.
18. **"Prerequisites are dropped on both sides."** Units only. `grep -rn "prerequisites:" data/buildings/` returns nothing: no building file authors the key, so on the structure side the field is inert end to end.
19. **"Two vehicles claim they need no factory."** Four: dir_cannon_tank, sod_shade_raider, dir_vanguard_car and dir_sentinel_scout.
20. **"The CY queue hash hole hides a divergence for up to 600 ticks."** BuildProgress and BuildPaid are hashed, so a head divergence normally surfaces within one tick. The genuinely invisible case is sharper and the audits missed it: factory and refinery are both Cost 2000 / BuildTicks 300, so a factory-versus-refinery divergence at the yard's queue head is bit-identical every tick until ReadyStructure is written. Real defect, wrong mechanism.
21. **"The stray `GetUnitType` at World.cs:1654 is harmless because the isCy branch returns first."** It is called unconditionally before the branch. It is harmless because World.cs:310 returns a defaulted struct rather than throwing. Anyone relying on the early return will still have executed the lookup.
22. **Assorted wrong line numbers that an implementer would follow:** `UnitCatalogue.ToTypeDef` is DataLoader.cs:194 and `StructureCatalogue.ToTypeDef` is :251, not both at 253; `StructureTypeDef` is World.cs:362, not 367; `_queues` is World.cs:317, not 318; footprint is schema.structure.json:25 and prerequisites is :27, not both at 27; the veil faction gate is World.cs:804, not 801. And "defence load 1.658 ms/tick" is a wall-clock benchmark quoted to three decimals as a finding; two other runs measured 1.638 and 1.637.

**Two claims from the audits are deliberately NOT dropped and are referred instead.** The legal finding that the GDD itself names the forbidden IP at lines 24, 29, 45, 83 and 92 (five lines, not the four one audit listed) and at docs/design/18-game-review-roadmap.md:126 is real: CLAUDE.md line 12 says "no exceptions, no exemptions for placeholders/tests/comments" and the GDD is source-of-truth number two. It should not be rated MINOR when the governing rule says no exceptions, and it is not this document's to fix. Two nuances for the Legal agent: line 92's occurrences sit inside a sentence instructing the team to AVOID resembling those brands, so it is a guardrail rather than an appropriation, and it should be rewritten to describe the palettes to avoid without naming their owners; and line 62's "Mammoth-slot" is not on the prohibition list literally, is arguably caught by the broader trade-dress clause, and is a judgement call that should be labelled as one rather than asserted alongside the certain hits. The mitigating reading throughout is that doc 00 line 50 scopes the ban to "the product" and doc 07 is deliberate competitor analysis, so internal design documents may be exempt by intent. CLAUDE.md as written does not say that. This wants a Legal call and a one-line scoping amendment, not a silent decision by an implementer or by this document.
